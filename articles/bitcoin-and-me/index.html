₿ bitcoin and me

a personal history

> magic internet money became a reason to learn.

I first heard about Bitcoin in 2016 from a friend.

At the time, I did not pay much attention to it. To me it was still “magic internet money”, some strange internet thing I did not really understand.

In 2018, Bitcoin came back to my attention because people were being very loud about the price charts.

But I was not looking for a trade.

The noise around the price made me want to understand what people were actually talking about. I wanted to know whether Bitcoin was only speculation, or whether there was something real behind it: a system with technical depth, economic meaning, and long-term resilience.

So I started digging.

the thing that clicked :

The idea that really clicked for me was proof of work.

Not only mining as a way to create blocks, but proof of work as a way to order events. A decentralized network of strangers can agree on a history, not because someone is in charge, but because rewriting that history requires real-world cost.

That felt insane to me.

It was the moment where Bitcoin stopped being “internet money” and became a serious technical object in my mind.

learning :

From there, Bitcoin became a reason to learn.

It made me study elliptic curve cryptography. On the way there I also looked at other digital signature schemes. It made me learn about hash functions, proof of work, stack-based languages, backward and forward compatibility, peer-to-peer networks, and software architecture.

It also made me think more seriously about freedom, censorship resistance, privacy, and what ownership means in a digital world.

contributing :

Once I became convinced that Bitcoin worked, and that it could keep working in the long term, I wanted to contribute in the ways I could.

I helped translate the Découvre Bitcoin website, now Plan B Network, into English. I helped review Bitcoin books such as L'Élégance de Bitcoin. I also contributed a bit through Bitcoin Stack Exchange.

building :

But most of my learning happened by building.

I implemented parts of the protocol. I wrote mnemonic generators. I parsed blockchain data. I looked at Bitcoin Core files on disk. I played with the peer-to-peer network. I wrote tools because I did not want to only use abstractions.

I wanted to see the thing myself.

A lot of that code was naive. Some of it still is. But I like keeping it around because it shows the path.

now :

I am still learning.

I still want to build, contribute, review, explain, and share as much as I can.

I hope I can spend my life building around this thing.
